Licensing

This library brings together fan-made WipEout art and design from several community contributors, plus in-game reference material. Each contributor released their work under different terms, verified from their own source pages. This page records those terms and what they mean for reuse.

Not legal advice. This is a good-faith reading of each source's stated terms for a non-commercial community/preservation project. If you intend to rely on any asset — especially commercially — confirm the licence with the original creator.

What these licences mean

The table above shows which licence applies to each contributor's work. Here's what each one permits for whatever asset you want to reuse:

  • CC BY-NC — you may share and adapt the work for non-commercial purposes, as long as you credit the creator.
  • CC BY-NC-SA — the same as CC BY-NC, plus share-alike: anything you build from it must be released under the same licence.
  • CC BY-NC-ND — NoDerivatives. You may share only verbatim, unmodified copies (non-commercial, with credit); you may not crop, split, recolour or otherwise adapt the work. Some creators pair this with an explicit "do not redistribute" request.
  • No formal licence — the creator granted no Creative Commons licence, so the work is technically "all rights reserved." Where such work appears here, the creator had publicly invited reuse (with credit); it's hosted in good faith for a non-commercial project and removed on request.

Across the board these are non-commercial terms: none of this material may be sold or used commercially without the original creator's permission.

How this library handles the art

  • Where the creator's licence allows it, marks are shared here directly — in both SVG (editable vector) and PNG (raster).
  • Where the licence is more restrictive, the vector isn't shared. The mark is shown as a lower-resolution PNG — a representation of the artwork only — with a link out to the creator's own page, where you can get the original vector from the source.

Each asset's licence is shown on its credit card on the site.
